Toward discovery. Toward local capability. Toward workers sharing the gain. Toward art with an honest lineage. Toward science that keeps the receipt. Toward communities that can refuse. Toward tools we can inspect, repair, fork, and leave. Toward an earth still alive enough to receive whatever future we keep bragging about.

I do not know how to solve the human condition. I do not know shit, and I am trying to stop turning that uncertainty into paralysis one day and authority the next.

I know the cage was built. Built things can be taken apart.

I know a black box can be opened without turning a person into glass.

I know a theorem can meet a kernel and a poem can stay free.

I know a confession is not a receipt. It can still be the first honest entry in a record that has been lying for a long time.

The Mad-Happy Scientist is still in there. Pissed off, tired, and waiting for me to stop asking whether I deserve to begin.

So get out of the mud. Not by pretending it is imaginary, and not by climbing onto somebody else's back. Get out, turn around, and put something down that gives the next person traction.

Pick the lock for everyone.

Let the walls rot.

Make room for something we could not imagine yesterday.

## The promise I can actually make

I believe this hard enough that I am afraid of failing it.

Even now, after writing all of this, I do not trust myself to live by every word for the rest of my life. I know my patterns. I know how conviction can burn hot and still lose to illness, appetite, exhaustion, shame, fear, or the next idea that makes everything else disappear. I still doubt myself. I am not going to clean that doubt out of the ending just to make the ending sound complete.

Mental health belongs in this conversation because no system built for people gets to pretend people are stable machines. Everybody needs some way back to themselves. Faith. Meditation. Therapy. Medication. Curiosity. Reflection. Movement. Art. Community. Maybe one of those. Maybe several. Find something that works for you, and let another person help hold it when you cannot hold it alone.

I cannot promise perfect behavior. That would be another lie wearing a promise.

What I can promise is that I stand by the conviction. I can construct a thought and a structure today that I will not tear down tomorrow just because I fail to live up to it. I can refuse to rewrite the principle to protect myself. I can leave the receipt and give other people the right to remind me what I said.

I want to build this for the people who endure, for the people we lost, and for the people who may not know whether they can endure what comes next. Not as a monument to suffering. As a handrail. A place to put weight down. A structure that does not require everybody to be strong at the same time.

Maybe I fail the conviction. Maybe I contradict it. Maybe somebody else has to carry it farther than I can.

The structure should survive that.

It should survive me.

I do not need this to become a doctrine. I just want to leave it here, intact, and let you sit with it.
