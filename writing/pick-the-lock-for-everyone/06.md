## What remains

AI is here, and hypocrisy was already here. We can keep arguing about which one is the greater contamination, or we can look at the structures surrounding both. Who owns the vehicle? Who chooses the direction? Who receives the gain? Who carries the exhaust? Who can inspect the route, object to the destination, and get out before the door locks?

Let go of the ethics that ask people to die proving they were pure. Keep the ethics that protect agency, consent, livelihood, relationship, accountability, and the earth. Stop mistaking a route for a destination, suffering for value, legality for justice, difficulty for meaning, or the robe for morality, and then aim the machinery somewhere useful: toward discovery. Toward local capability. Toward workers sharing the gain. Toward art with an honest lineage. Toward science that keeps its receipts. Toward communities that can refuse. Toward tools we can inspect, repair, fork, and leave. Toward an earth still alive enough to receive whatever future we keep bragging about.

I do not know how to solve the human condition. I do not know much at the scale where people start pretending to have final answers, and I am trying to stop turning that uncertainty into paralysis one day and authority the next.

I know the cage was built. Built things can be taken apart.

I know a black box can be opened without turning a person into glass.

I know a theorem can meet a kernel and a poem can stay free.

I know a confession is not a receipt. It can still be the first honest entry in a record that has been lying for a long time.

The Mad-Happy Scientist is still in there. Tired, impatient, angry, curious, and waiting for me to stop asking whether I deserve to begin.

So get out of the mud. Not by pretending it is imaginary, and not by climbing onto somebody else's back. Get out, turn around, and put something down that gives the next person traction.

Pick the lock for everyone.

Let the walls rot.

Make room for something we could not imagine yesterday.
