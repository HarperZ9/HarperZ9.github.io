## I Am a Living, Breathing Reaction

I am not a clean origin point. Nobody is.

I am a living, breathing reaction: to my father, my mother, my stepfather, my brother, my peers, the women I loved and hurt, the men I copied, the rooms I survived, the institutions I distrusted, the music that taught me how to posture, the art that taught me how to ache, the drugs I used to leave myself, the code that let me build another world, and every person who showed me kindness when I had not yet learned how to return it.

Environment enters. I transform it. Consequence leaves.

That is not an acquittal. A reaction still has products. People still breathe what comes out of the chamber. The point is that transformation is real, and if the inputs, constraints, catalysts, and feedback change, the reaction can change too.

That is part of why I think AI can become what sampling became for music.

Sampling did not prove that musicians had run out of ideas. It proved that culture was already recursive. A drum break could leave one room, cross a decade, meet a machine, a neighborhood, a different grief, and become something that did not exist before. The sampler was not the whole act. Selection mattered. Timing mattered. Chopping mattered. Context mattered. The person hearing a possibility inside an old sound mattered.

The ugliest part was never the fact that sound could be transformed. It was the ownership system around the masters: the labels, clearance regimes, distribution choke points, contracts, and people who made themselves indispensable by standing between artists and one another.

AI is rebuilding the record label around the sampler.

The model can become an instrument. The source graph can preserve lineage. Artists can declare what entered, what was licensed, what was refused, what was transformed, and who should receive credit or an agreed share. Communities can own the tools, the relationships, and the treasury. A steward can coordinate without becoming the landlord of the culture moving through the system.

Fuck the label. Keep the lineage.

And this does not stop at art. Every domain has its record labels: journals that confuse distribution with authority, credentials that confuse access with merit, banks that confuse coordination with ownership, cloud platforms that confuse hosting with a permanent claim on capability, bureaucracies that turn a necessary boundary into a hereditary class of people allowed to speak.

The point is not to erase expertise, consent, attribution, privacy, or payment. The point is to stop allowing the coordinator to own the whole reaction.

I know the language I inherited for defiance. The music taught me lines like, "ima fuck yo bitch." I understand the voltage. It is conquest compressed into a sentence: I can enter your world, take what you call yours, and make your authority look weak.

But listen to the property claim inside it.

A woman is not evidence that I defeated another man. She is not a territory, a receipt of conquest, or a supporting character in a performance of masculinity. If I am trying to dismantle ownership, I cannot keep reaching for a sentence that turns a woman into property whenever I want to sound powerful.

So I want to keep the defiance and kill the possession.

Fuck the monopoly, not the woman. Fuck the gate, not the person trapped beside it. Fuck the label, the landlord, the lie, and the little pipe they expect everybody else to drink from.

Come get me means: inspect the work. Contradict me. Rebuild it. Fork it. Show me the receipt that proves the principle fails. I do not need to threaten a body to stand behind an idea.

My principle is fertile. Let it impregnate the culture, not possess a person. Breed it. Fork it. Mutate it. Give birth to something better than me. Let it reproduce because people choose to carry it, because it gives them leverage, food, time, dignity, or a way to make something they could not make before.

I may still use the old language. I may still fall into the old posture. The record already shows that knowing better has never guaranteed that I behave better.

But I want to learn to call women women.

I want to see myself as a man.

Not because manhood gives me a license to dominate, collect, conquer, or disappear when the bill arrives. I want a manhood built around provision without possession, protection without control, strength that can hear no, and the ability to remain present after the explanation stops working.

I want to provide something good.

That does not make me good. Sincerity is not a receipt. The work has to survive contact with another person's reality. It has to be useful without requiring faith in me. It has to preserve an exit when I become the obstacle. It has to let somebody take the principle farther than I can, change it, reject me, and still keep what helped.

I am a living, breathing reaction.

That means I carry what came before me.

It also means the reaction is still happening.
