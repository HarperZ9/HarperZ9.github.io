Local-first tools. Smaller models made more capable through good scaffolding, memory, routing, and verification instead of constant dependence on the largest private cluster. Portable histories. Open protocols. Community-owned compute. Cooperative licensing. Data trusts. Labor agreements that return productivity gains to workers. The right to run offline. The right to inspect, repair, fork, and leave.

Receipts instead of badges.

A badge says, "Trust me, I belong here." A receipt gives you something to walk.

That is what Project Telos is trying to become. Gather the source. Keep the boundary. Generate candidates. Separate worker and verifier. Record the causal path. Preserve the failure. Let a claim shrink when the witness cannot carry it. Make the system say `DRIFT` or `UNVERIFIABLE` without treating uncertainty as an embarrassing hole to fill with bullshit.

I am not a priest of truth. I do not know shit at the scale of the world. I am trying to create an environment where nobody, especially me, gets to turn certainty into authority without opening the work.

The earth belongs inside that environment too.

There is no liberation in burning through the planet to create infinite disposable content. Data centers are physical. They consume electricity, water, land, metals, chips, transmission capacity, and public infrastructure. The cloud is somebody else's building sitting on somebody's grid and drawing from somebody's watershed.

If the creative future depends on invisible sacrifice zones, then the cage has not disappeared. It moved.

We need compute budgets, energy disclosure, repairable hardware, longer-lived systems, and an honest preference for the smaller tool when the smaller tool can do the job. Local-first is not automatically green either. Nothing gets a moral pass because it sits under a desk instead of behind a corporate fence.

AI can help search materials, model biology, explore mathematics, manage energy systems, and show structures we missed. It can also convert the biosphere into synthetic sludge nobody remembers asking for.

The direction still belongs to us. That is the problem and the hope.

## What I owe the Mad-Happy Scientist

I keep returning to that kid because I think a lot of people have one.

Maybe yours was an artist, astronaut, mechanic, naturalist, writer, musician, teacher, healer, or the person who took apart every broken object in the house to see whether failure had a shape.

Then life happened. Some people jumped through the hoops and made it. Some tripped. Some were pushed out. Some turned left. Some found a job that paid rent and slowly separated them from the part of themselves that asked questions for no reason besides wonder.

I treated the missed road into formal research as a verdict. I let pedigree become metaphysics. The institution had a map, and I decided any territory outside it must be imaginary.

Then I resented the institution for a cage I was helping maintain.

That resentment fed the grandiosity. Every project had to prove everything. Every good week had to become an empire. Every new tool needed to repay every lost year immediately. The bipolar tendencies I see in myself can turn light into appetite. I find something real and want all of it: more capability, more projects, more proof, more impact, more recognition, every door open at once.

The structure outruns me. I collapse. People around me inherit the debris.

I keep trying to swallow the sun and acting surprised when it burns.

Grandiosity and shame are close relatives. One says I deserve the keys to the city. The other says I am too corrupt to enter. Both keep the world organized around me. Ordinary work starts feeling too small, which is convenient because ordinary work is where accountability lives.

One test that can fail. One claim another person can inspect. One tool that runs on the machine somebody owns. One artist who names the lineage. One apology made without controlling the response. One boundary accepted even though it hurts. One door left open behind you.

I still want to pick the lock. I probably always will.

The change is that I do not want to pick it only for myself. I want to open the cage for everybody, throw away the key, and let the walls rot into soil.

That sounds beautiful. It should also make you suspicious. People promising universal liberation have worn a lot of robes.

So contradict me. Inspect the system. Reject the doctrine. Fork the tool. Leave the platform. Protect your interior. Tell me the argument breaks. Nothing I build gets to inherit authority from the fact that I hate authority.

I used to want a killing blow against my ego. Even that can become theater. There is no clean terminal command for the self. The ego does not need root access, but I cannot delete it and call the deletion repair.

Self-destruction removes the person who owes the work.

At sixteen I thought disappearance might solve the contradiction of being alive inside myself. Later I found slower ways to disappear: drugs, fights, code, isolation, ambition, fantasy, collapse, and the performance of hating myself so thoroughly that nobody could ask for anything more specific.

I am done using annihilation as evidence that I understand the harm.

The apology has to be less cinematic. Tell the truth before exposure forces it. Correct the record. Return what can be returned. Pay the cost you moved. Accept another person's boundary without turning it into a referendum on your worth. Listen without editing their account into something easier to survive. Do not demand forgiveness as the prize for finally admitting what happened. Put friction around the behavior that good intentions have repeatedly failed to stop.

Then continue, because repair needs somebody alive to do it.

My mistakes are part of the record. They inform the risk. They do not get to write every future verdict alone.

I am not asking to be seen as good. I am trying to become answerable.

Maybe that is the human thing I was looking for beneath all of this. Not purity. Not a protected list of tasks a machine can never do. The ability to owe. To be contradicted. To change without deleting the record. To care what our actions do inside another life. To remain present after the explanation runs out.

AI is here. Hypocrisy is here too. We can keep arguing about which one arrived first, or we can look inward and start building systems that make it harder for either to rule without appeal.

Let go of the archaic ethics that ask people to die proving they were pure. Keep the ethics that protect agency, consent, livelihood, relation, accountability, and the earth. Stop mistaking a route for a destination. Stop mistaking suffering for value. Stop mistaking legality for justice. Stop mistaking the robe for morality.

Aim the machinery somewhere useful.
