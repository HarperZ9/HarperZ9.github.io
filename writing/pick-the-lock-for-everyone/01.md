# Pick the Lock for Everyone

## An apology to the Mad-Happy Scientist, an argument about the cages we inherit and rebuild, and a case for accountable creation after AI

*Zain Dana Harper, July 2026*

> **Content note:** This essay discusses suicidal depression, dissociation, substance use, violence, misogyny, shame, and harm to other people. It does not describe methods.

I have never apologized.

Not really.

I have said *sorry* plenty of times. I have also explained myself until the explanation became the only thing left in the room. I have collapsed under the weight of what I did, called myself a piece of shit with enough conviction that somebody else eventually had to stop being angry and start taking care of me, and then mistaken that reversal for honesty. I have disappeared into work, substances, code, a new project, a new emergency, a new argument about why the world is arranged badly. Some of those arguments were true, which was part of what made them so useful to me; a true explanation can still be used like a fake passport.

When I was sixteen, I was suicidal, depressed, dissociating, acting out, and swallowing almost everything I did not know how to say. I almost died from drugs, fights, self-criticism, and the kind of reckless momentum that can feel strangely calm from inside it. I hurt people physically, mentally, and spiritually. I cheated on tests. I made video game cheats. I lied my way around consequences. I learned to find the loose board in the fence and became proud of how quickly I could get through it.

There was always another reason I was the exception. Another private court where my pain entered as evidence and everybody else's pain was treated like an accusation I had the right to cross-examine.

I know how severe that sounds. I also know how easy it is for severity to become another kind of performance, so let me slow down and say what I mean.

The drinking and the drugs are not the center of this story. Neither is the fact that I avoided things. Those are visible symbols of a deeper pattern: I wanted to confront something in myself, badly, but I had not learned how to do that without either turning the confrontation into punishment or finding a way to escape it. I wanted relief, growth, discovery, and a better life. At the same time, I refused myself the patience and vulnerability that growth actually requires. I would demand transformation from myself, immediately, and then treat the failure to transform on command as proof that I was beyond repair.

Substances offered a crude imitation of movement. For a few hours the pressure in the room shifted, and I could believe I had shifted with it, but the original structure was still there in the morning, usually with more debris around it.

That does not make the substances innocent, and it does not make me their victim in a way that removes my agency. It means they belonged to a longing I did not know how to approach directly. They were one of the ways I kept asking for a different life while denying myself the space to build one. The other part of that pattern was something I used to call independence.

I distrusted authority from a young age. Some of that distrust was taught to me, and some of it was earned. I did not believe institutions had my interests at heart. I did not believe the official route was necessarily the honest one. I did not want to build systems that made existing authority stronger, so I built systems in support of myself, and then in support of the people near me: you, your aunt, your cousins, whoever was standing outside the same gate.

There is something good in that instinct. There is also something I did not want to look at.

If independence means carving a path for yourself while the people who love you carry the cost of the carving, then it is not independence. It is learned helplessness with a sharper tool. I refused to depend on people honestly, and then depended on them to survive the consequences of that refusal. I wanted to be self-made while quietly using their time, money, patience, sleep, and concern as the floor beneath the performance.

Relationships are not transactions. Love is not an invoice. Still, when another person makes a decision with you in mind, they give something up. Maybe it is time. Maybe it is an opportunity, a night's sleep, a quieter month, a little more of their faith than they can comfortably afford. Respecting that sacrifice is not transactional, while pretending it does not count can become exploitation. I have often recognized that too late, usually because my thoughts move quickly when I am in motion. Pride, shame, victimhood, resentment, tenderness, and a fairly predatory view of humanity can all be arguing over the steering wheel before I have decided what I actually believe. I take the fastest neural route available, say the thing with the most voltage, and then spend days doing relationship maintenance and emotional janitorial work. I explain what I meant. I repair the part that landed wrong. I reassure people that the worst version of the sentence was not the whole of me. Meanwhile the original issue is still sitting there, waiting for attention, and I am falling further behind on the ordinary give and take that any relationship needs.

Profanity is sometimes useful to me because it breaks through ceremony. Sometimes one word carries the frustration honestly. Other times it is compression. I reach for the strongest word because I have not given myself enough time to understand the thought underneath it. My speaking voice does that more than my writing voice. The internal monologue changes when it is moving, and not every fast route deserves to become a public road. Self-condemnation can be another fast route. I can say, "I am a piece of shit, and I probably will be tomorrow," and there is truth in the fact that one essay does not change my habits by morning. But that sentence can still become a shortcut. It can flatten every specific harm into one total identity, and then there is nothing left to repair because the case has already been closed. Shame gets to feel deep while doing very little work.

I am not asking for release from judgment. I am not asking the reader to replace consequences with sympathy because I finally found language for the pattern. I want to sit here. Then I want to stand up, walk out where I can be seen, and accept that some people may make a mockery of me. I have spent years trying to control that possibility. I have lied, redirected, disappeared, charmed, become angry, become fragile, or become too busy whenever the room began arriving at a verdict I could not edit. That is not accountability; it is fear with an architectural plan.

Somewhere inside that architecture was the child I used to imagine becoming. I called him the Mad-Happy Scientist.

He was not thinking about prestige. He wanted research, graphics, code, biology, mathematics, physics, strange instruments, and the feeling of asking a question nobody had answered yet. He wanted to take apart the broken thing and see whether the failure had a shape. He wanted to be wrong in an interesting direction and laugh when something finally moved.

By my teenage years, I was chasing prestige anyway. Prestige was not the dream. Acceptance was. I was low enough that recognition from almost anybody could feel like evidence that I existed. I found belonging among people who were struggling too, and what I learned there was mixed: real friendship, humor, music, survival knowledge, cruelty, tenderness, misogyny, defiance, and a version of manhood that often confused possession with strength.

I listened to Mac Dre, Andre Nickatina, RJmrLA, Kodak Black, the Allman Brothers Band, Radiohead, R.E.M., Pink Floyd, and other artists because they let contradiction remain audible: swagger beside grief, beauty beside alienation, humor beside despair. I was not looking for a coherent doctrine. I was looking for proof that a person could contain incompatible things and still make something that reached another person.

*Jacob's Ladder* stayed with me. So did *Platoon*, Dali, Beksinski, Bessel van der Kolk, Robert Sapolsky, Bill Nye, *Sesame Street*, and the substances I used to leave myself. It is not a respectable syllabus. It is the one I had. War, trauma, behavior, children's science, puppets teaching decency, impossible landscapes, music about appetite and loss, and chemicals that briefly changed the temperature of the room all entered the same young nervous system.

I do not list those things to make my history sound more interesting. I list them because environment matters, and because saying that environment matters is not the same as saying environment is guilty in my place. Culture did not borrow my hands without permission, and I absorbed a counterfeit version of manhood and repeated enough of it to hurt people. I learned to talk about women as objects, trophies, proof that one man had defeated another. I learned that bullying could be called strength and exploitation could be called survival. I also knew, somewhere underneath the posture, that this was wrong. That tension did not automatically correct me. Often it made me swing harder between the person I wanted to be and the person who could get what he wanted fastest.

I have said that I want to learn to call women women, and I mean something plain by that. I do not want a woman reduced to evidence that one man defeated another, or to a territory inside somebody else's performance of masculinity. I also want to see myself as a man without using manhood as a license to dominate. The version I am trying to reach looks more like provision without possession, protection without control, strength that can hear no, and the ability to stay present after the explanation stops working. I am not claiming I already live there. I am naming the direction because the older map did real damage.

I have called myself a living, breathing reaction, and I still mean it, though I need to be careful with the phrase. Environment enters. I transform it. Consequences leave. That is a description of a process, not an acquittal. People still breathe what comes out of the chamber.

The important part is that a reaction is not finished while it is still occurring. Conditions can change. Catalysts can be removed. New constraints can be introduced. The output can become something else. None of that changes what was produced before.

It only means the record is not the same thing as destiny.

For years, I treated the turn away from formal research as a permanent sentence. I kept the dream alive as a moonlighting operation. Every late-night repository became evidence that I had not really abandoned it. Every unfinished tool was a promissory note to some future version of me who would finally become consistent. Every burst of energy became the beginning of the life I was supposed to have, and when the burst collapsed I used the wreckage to prove I had failed again.

My family and the people I love paid for that private war. They paid in worry, money, interrupted sleep, neglect, unfinished promises, and the exhausting uncertainty of whether the light I found this week would become something real or another fire I expected them to stand beside.

So I am trying to apologize, first to that kid.

I am sorry I abandoned you and then used your name as cover. I am sorry I turned curiosity into a weapon I could use against myself. I am sorry I kept saying we were almost there while walking in circles and blaming the walls.

I am sorry to the people I love for making them carry a war I refused to name, for demanding faith while refusing consistency, and for acting as if my pain was the only evidence that mattered.

This essay is not the apology they are owed. That has to happen directly, privately, and without an audience. They get to be angry. They get to refuse me. They get to remember the events differently. They do not owe me absolution because I finally managed to put the pattern into public language.

A confession can be another shortcut. I know because shortcuts are one of the few fields where I have extensive experience.

## The first black box I learned to exploit

Why begin an essay about artificial intelligence here?

Because I am tired of hearing people talk about black boxes as if opacity arrived with neural networks.

People are black boxes with excellent public relations. We make a decision, feel the decision, and then hire language to defend it. We call appetite principle, fear discernment, status merit, property ethics, obedience professionalism, and avoidance self-care. We call a legal outcome moral because the law happened to protect the person already holding the keys.

I did the same thing on a smaller surface, at home, where it hurt people I claimed to love.

When I lied, I was not just moving a few words around. I was editing another person's picture of reality without their permission and then benefiting from what they did inside the picture I gave them. When I cheated, I was changing the evidence other people used to decide who had done the work, who understood it, who deserved trust, or who should receive an opportunity.

I controlled the input and then acted offended when anybody questioned the output.

That is a small autocracy. Nothing glamorous about it. I hated authority and wore a little crown in private.

I still lie. I still avoid. I am still capable of charm, anger, collapse, or intellectual distance when I should sit down and listen. Writing that does not make me honest. It is closer to a risk disclosure, and it is also where the programs come from. They are not my redemption. They are not evidence that I have become accountable, and I do not want them used as a technical indulgence issued in place of an apology. They are a confrontation built out of the material I know how to use. What are the tools supposed to do, then? Not save me, and certainly not testify that I am a good person. They are supposed to make some of my favorite evasions more difficult. Crucible takes a thesis apart and asks what would refute each claim. Forum preserves a causal record of what agents did. EMET witnesses the bytes instead of treating a signature as moral authority. Gather tries to keep the source boundary visible. Learn keeps the human involved because I do not want a machine that makes me faster while I become less capable of understanding what I am doing.

A person can build a smoke alarm and still light the house on fire. I know that. The point is not that the tools prove something good about me. The point is that the work should not require goodness from the person who built it.

A system built by a liar should be able to catch the liar.

A system built by an egotist should survive his loss of control.

A system built by somebody desperate for recognition should continue working when his name is removed.

I do not want the artifact entering the room before I do and negotiating a better verdict on my behalf. An idea is not me. A brick is not the hand that set it down. A principle does not become true because a decent person spoke it, and it does not become false because a hypocrite found it first.

The question is whether the brick bears weight. Can another person inspect it? Can they move it, test it, replace it, or use it to build a surface I did not imagine? Can the system preserve a useful claim while rejecting the person who made it? Can it protect somebody from me?

If the work requires admiration, it is another insecurity machine.

If it can indict me, outlive me, and remain useful to people who never need to trust me, then it may be infrastructure.

That is the inversion I care about. The creator's confession cannot warrant the creation, and the creation cannot absolve the creator.
