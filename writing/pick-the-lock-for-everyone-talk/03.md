Local-first tools. Smaller models made more capable through good scaffolding and verification. Portable histories. Open protocols. Community-owned compute. Cooperative licensing. Labor agreements that return productivity gains to workers. The right to run offline. The right to inspect, repair, fork, and leave.

Receipts instead of badges.

A badge says, "Trust me, I belong here." A receipt gives you something to walk.

The earth belongs inside the receipt too. The cloud is somebody else's building, sitting on somebody's grid, drawing from somebody's watershed. If the creative future depends on invisible sacrifice zones, the cage did not disappear. It moved.

[Slow down.]

I keep returning to the Mad-Happy Scientist because I think a lot of people have one.

Maybe yours was an artist, astronaut, mechanic, naturalist, teacher, writer, musician, healer, or the kid who took apart every broken object in the house to see whether failure had a shape.

Then life happened. Some people jumped through the hoops. Some tripped. Some were pushed out. Some turned left. Some found a job that paid the rent and slowly separated them from the part of themselves that asked questions for no reason besides wonder.

I treated the missed road into formal research as a verdict. I let pedigree become metaphysics.

Then I resented the institution for a cage I was helping maintain.

That resentment fed grandiosity. Every project had to prove everything. Every good week had to become an empire. I kept trying to swallow the sun and acting surprised when it burned.

Grandiosity and shame are close relatives. One says I deserve the keys to the city. The other says I am too corrupt to enter. Both keep the whole world organized around me.

Ordinary accountable work is smaller.

One test that can fail. One claim another person can inspect. One artist who names the lineage. One apology made without controlling the response. One boundary accepted even though it hurts. One door left open behind you.

I still want to pick the lock. I probably always will.

The change is that I do not want to pick it only for myself.

I want to open the cage for everybody, throw away the key, and let the walls rot into soil.

That should make you suspicious. People promising universal liberation have worn a lot of robes.

So contradict me. Inspect the system. Reject the doctrine. Fork the tool. Leave the platform. Tell me the argument breaks. Nothing I build gets authority from the fact that I hate authority.

I used to want a killing blow against my ego. Even that can be theater. There is no terminal command for the self. Self-destruction removes the person who owes the work.

The apology has to be less cinematic. Tell the truth before exposure forces it. Correct the record. Pay the cost you moved. Accept another person's boundary. Listen without editing their account. Do not demand forgiveness as the prize for finally admitting what happened.

Then continue, because repair needs somebody alive to do it.

[Final rise. Do not rush.]

AI is here. Hypocrisy is here too.

Let go of the old ethics that ask people to die proving they were pure. Keep the ethics that protect agency, consent, livelihood, relation, accountability, and the earth.

Aim the machinery somewhere useful.

Toward discovery. Toward local capability. Toward workers sharing the gain. Toward art with an honest lineage. Toward science that keeps the receipt. Toward communities that can refuse. Toward tools we can inspect, repair, fork, and leave.

I do not know how to solve the human condition. I do not know shit, and I am trying to stop turning that uncertainty into paralysis one day and authority the next.

I know the cage was built. Built things can be taken apart.

I know a black box can be opened without turning a person into glass.

I know a theorem can meet a kernel and a poem can stay free.

I know a confession is not a receipt. It can still be the first honest entry in a record that has been lying for a long time.

The Mad-Happy Scientist is still in there. Pissed off, tired, and waiting for me to stop asking whether I deserve to begin.

So get out of the mud. Do not pretend it is imaginary. Do not climb onto somebody else's back.

Get out, turn around, and put something down that gives the next person traction.

Pick the lock for everyone.

Let the walls rot.

Make room for something we could not imagine yesterday.

[Drop the rise. Speak plainly.]

I need to end with something less heroic.

I believe this hard enough that I am afraid of failing it.

I do not trust myself to live by every word I just said for the rest of my life. I know my patterns. I know what illness, shame, appetite, exhaustion, and fear can do. I still doubt myself. I am not going to clean that doubt out of the ending.

Mental health matters for everybody. Find a way back to yourself that actually works for you. Faith. Meditation. Therapy. Medication. Curiosity. Reflection. Art. Community. Maybe several of them. Let somebody help hold the door open when you cannot.

I should be honest about my route. I do not do well with one-on-one therapy. I still prefer self-medication more often than I should. That is not advice. It is part of the record.

Sometimes refusing an institution is self-knowledge. Sometimes it is avoidance wearing self-determination as a costume. A path your spirit did not choose can become another cage. A self-chosen path can still walk you straight into a wall.

Help is not surrender. Autonomy is not immunity. I have to ask whether the route makes me more present, less destructive, and more able to answer for what I do.

I said I like to sit in my pain. The honest answer is that I do a bit of both.

Sometimes I sit beside it. Sometimes I sit inside it and call the punishment reflection. I have made a home there before. I know the furniture.

I own that.

I am also beginning to learn how to love myself again.

I believe everybody deserves redemption. Not erasure. Not automatic forgiveness. Redemption.

Sometimes the bridge over the canyon fails and you go tumbling down. You do not wake up back on the road. You still have to get up and make the slow march out of the canyon.

Maybe you crawl for part of it. Maybe somebody walks beside you.

But you move.

Otherwise you are just a memory of yesterday.

[Stay plain. This is not a savior speech.]

I want to swallow the darkness and release light in the form of spiritual and mental sincerity.

I think about *The Green Mile*. The fantasy of taking pain out of somebody, holding it long enough to change its direction, and leaving something gentler behind.

I understand why that image stays with me. I spent a lot of my life taking pain in, mixing it with my own, and throwing it back into the room in a worse shape.

I do not want martyrdom to become another escape. Nobody should have to absorb the whole world's pain until they disappear. The gift has to be a thought, a tool, a structure, a place where the next person does not have to carry the same weight alone.

So, to the young people, the young men and women, and anybody else listening: do not take me as an example of how to behave.

I repeated the same mistakes as people before me. I made the same excuses and found better language for making them. I took what I received from my father, my mother, my stepfather, my brother, my peers, my exes, and every room that taught me how to survive. I internalized it, distorted it, and sometimes handed it to somebody else.

My behavior reflects that environment. It is not the whole of my intention. It is still my behavior.

Environment is the medium, not an acquittal. It shapes a person, an animal, an idea, a song, a wound, a criticism. That is also why transformation is possible. Change the conditions. Keep the record. Make another choice. Something else can grow.

Until somebody dies, another move remains. Even after a life ends, the impact keeps moving. Harm can stay harm and still teach somebody where the cliff is.

I believe in everybody. Even people who cannot believe in themselves.

I believe in you.

I am trying to believe in me.

I am not announcing an exit. I am here. I stand behind the idea. I am putting it down because anything I want to survive a mood has to leave my head.

Record it. Archive it. Let people argue with it. Then turn it toward something material. Healing. Learning. Art. Discovery. Food. Rent. Time. People getting paid enough to care for the people they love.

Everybody is only a few decisions, a few losses, or one missing hand away from the person beside them they judge.

Desperation is not a character test.

The middleman can be a regular man too. A steward at the table instead of the owner of it. We can make the thing, share the gain, and leave enough behind for somebody else to begin.

I said a sincere intention to do good cannot be tarnished. I know what I mean. I also have to apply the same rule here that I apply everywhere else.

Sincerity is not a receipt.

A good intention can be contradicted by behavior. It can become another robe. The gift begins when sincerity survives contact with somebody else's reality and becomes something they can use without having to trust me.

That is what I want to leave behind.

I cannot promise perfect behavior. That would be another lie wearing a promise.

I can promise that I stand by the conviction. I can build a thought and a structure today that I will not tear down tomorrow to protect my ego. I can leave something other people can inspect, criticize, use, and carry farther than I can.

I want to build it for the people who endure, for the people we lost, and for the people who may not know whether they can endure what comes next.

Not a monument.

A handrail.

A place to put some weight down.

Maybe I fail this conviction. Maybe I contradict it. Maybe one of you has to remind me what I said.

The structure should survive that.

It should survive me.

[Pause.]

I just wanted to leave that here.

Sit with it.

[Stop. Let the silence hold before leaving.]

## Delivery notes

The opening should sound exposed, not theatrical. Do not rush the first pause and do not ask the audience to reassure you.

The music and masculinity passage is not a defense and not a playlist interlude. The turn lands on "Culture did not borrow my hands without permission." Leave the uncertainty around bipolarity intact; do not diagnose yourself onstage.

The middle is an argument, not a confession marathon. Increase pace through the hypocrisy section, then release the pressure when the graph and source-bounded art appear. That turn should feel like curiosity returning.

The final rise should not become the ending. Let it crest, then drop the performance register completely for the mental-health coda. The last lines are not a claim of redemption or strength. They are an admission that the conviction may need to outlive the person who formulated it.

Do not turn the canyon into an inspirational image. It should sound tired, physical, and ordinary. Pause after "Otherwise you are just a memory of yesterday." Do not punch the line.

The gift passage is not a savior speech. Do not perform *The Green Mile* image as martyrdom, and do not preach at younger listeners. The load-bearing turn is "Sincerity is not a receipt." The gift is the structure, not the suffering.

The strongest optional cut for a shorter venue is the detailed art-instrument paragraph beginning "The artist chooses the primitives." The steward-platform, parallel-infrastructure, and open-failure movements can also be removed while preserving the personal arc. Keep the final coda unless the venue requires a hard stop.
