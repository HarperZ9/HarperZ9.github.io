There is another thing I want to build, because criticism alone does not feed anybody.

Artists already work through references, moodboards, collages, shared folders, and chains of influence. What if the creative workspace kept a living source graph for the things a person actually brought into the room?

Not a plagiarism cop. Not a machine trying to calculate who owns twelve percent of a brushstroke.

A record. This photograph. That poster. This palette. That composition. The earlier artist's name, link, license, and preferred credit stay attached instead of disappearing the second their work enters somebody else's process.

If the final work earns money, the people in that declared chain could receive a share when the relationship is clear and everybody agreed to it.

The analogy in my head is a modlist that can assemble a world without pretending it authored every mod inside it. Coordination is real work. Coordination does not require erasure.

The platform also needs a place where people can settle differences without immediately turning one another into enemies. Show the process. Correct the record. Offer credit. Remove the source. Negotiate a license. Disagree. Maybe collaborate later.

I want a steward, not another thief standing between the artist and the audience.

The platform should not own the work, the identity, the social graph, or the relationship. If it charges, the fee should be visible and tied to something it actually did. People should be able to leave with their records and their community intact.

The same thing applies to every service platform I care about.

Its success should be measured partly by how much time it gives back.

Did the system save the user ten hours? Fine. Who received those ten hours?

If the person gets two and the company turns eight into margin, the system did not return time. It extracted a productivity gain and put liberation on the invoice.

I want a time dividend. The person gets to keep the hour for rest, family, study, art, or doing absolutely nothing. The platform does not get to call life "unused capacity" and sell it again.

Maybe we can all eat if we stop hoarding the food.
