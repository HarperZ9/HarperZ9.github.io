PHOTOGRAPHY BY IAMBETA

The living world, up close.

Every algorithm in the atelier is modeled on something real — a spiral, a vein, the way a seed packs itself in. These are the originals: macro photographs by iamBeTa, whose eye on the small living world is where all of this started. Look closely — then, on the ones the engine knows, let it read the photograph back to you as a drawing.

And the engine on top.

On four of these, you can watch the atelier read the photograph the way a person learns to look — it sees the light, the edges, the way the structure flows — and then draws something new along those lines. Her photograph becomes the field; the algorithm becomes the pen. Nothing is guessed: the drawing is deterministic from a stated seed, and it carries a witness of its own geometry, so you can re-derive it instead of trust it.
