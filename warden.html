WARDEN overview

A review layer for AI-assisted work.

WARDEN is a provenance and review system for authorized AI-assisted work. It carries scope, approvals, evidence, and constraints into the workflow, then carries actions, claims, receipts, and reviewer notes back out.

Confidential research membrane

Proof and permission belong at the work boundary.

01
Scope into the model

WARDEN carries the authorization context inward.

For long-term red-team contracts, biological research, government-supervised defense or weapons-adjacent engineering, confidential product review, and sensitive internal R&D, WARDEN acts as a programmatic assertion and provenance layer: engagement scope, approvals, constraints, source documents, and exception notes are made visible where the model is asked to work.

engagement scopeapproval recordconstraintsexception notes
02
Receipts back to the operator

WARDEN carries the work record outward.

WARDEN does not grant authority by itself. It preserves the operator-owned proof, scope, and documentation, then records model edits, actions, claims, release gates, provenance checks, anomalies, and reviewer handoff notes so authorized work can be inspected after the fact.

model editsaction ledgerprovenance checksreview trail

Public context: the OpenAI Frontier Governance Framework names cyber, CBRN, manipulation, loss-of-control, incident-response, and external-review concerns; the Anthropic Responsible Scaling Policy discusses catastrophic-risk governance, biological science capabilities, and automated R&D; and Anthropic's June 12, 2026 statement says a U.S. export-control directive led it to disable Fable 5 and Mythos 5. WARDEN is the architecture this project is building toward: explicit scope, visible authorization context, accountable model work, and reviewable receipts.

Creative trust

Safety, transparency, and creativity can evolve on the same surface.

01
Creative hesitation

That hesitation belongs in the architecture.

Many creative people are hesitant to use AI tools, and some reject them outright. That hesitation belongs in the architecture. AI touches authorship, labor, consent, attribution, taste, safety, and control at the same time; pretending otherwise makes the tools harder to trust.

authorshipconsentattributioncontrol
02
Machine sensing

The problem underneath is state.

The problem underneath is state. A model can infer, summarize, and remember, but it usually receives claims about the world rather than native contact with the world. WARDEN-style programmatic sensory organs give models a safer surface to observe, act, and leave receipts.

live stateprogrammatic sensory organsreceiptshuman review

The same surface that makes machine creativity safer to trust is also the surface that keeps it accountable as capabilities grow. WARDEN is not an argument that models already know ground truth; it is a way to put scope, state, proof, and review closer to the place where people and machines meet.

Public/private boundary

Show the method. Protect the core.

WARDEN has a larger private body of work. The public page shows the leaves that can be inspected and the human accountability pattern behind them. It does not publish private internals, credentials, client data, operational details, or sensitive workflows. It names the scope honestly because confidential work still needs provenance.

Position

A way to keep capability from outrunning authorization.

WARDEN is useful when AI-assisted work needs a review trail: engagement scope, authorization context, live-state observations, agent intent, action ledgers, claims, release gates, evidence, provenance, anomalies, witness receipts, controlled exceptions, and human ownership in one line of sight. The public repos show pieces of that pattern; they do not ask the reader to accept the private core on faith.
