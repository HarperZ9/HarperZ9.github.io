A witness is not an authority.

EMET is the witness anchor in the proof-surface stack. It checks whether bytes and presented views still match what they claim to represent, then emits a restrained verdict instead of asking for trust.

No TRUSTED verdict

A witness, not an authority.

Use it when a source and a generated copy need a witness: compare the exact bytes, report any drift, and hand the result to a reviewer.

Boundary: it reports a comparison; it does not decide whether a system is safe or trustworthy.
