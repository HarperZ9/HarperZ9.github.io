Research program

Senses and Sensibility

A falsifiable theory of intrinsic, bilateral accountability — and the philosophical corpus it is extracted from.

Zain Dana Harper/ Seattle · 2025–2026/ MIT-licensed · authored · dated/ github.com/HarperZ9

Abstract

Agentic systems perceive, decide, and act — but their claims about the world, and their authority to change it, are asserted, not answerable to evidence. This program argues that accountability is not an external audit bolted on after the fact but an intrinsic, bilateral property a system can be built to have: every observation carries provenance and can fail a self-test; every action passes a gate that can only allow, deny, or escalate to a human; and the same evidentiary standard binds the maker, not only the machine. The theory is stated falsifiably, instantiated in a working proof-of-concept, and honest about its own status — pre-proof, with a stated path to a law.

The thesis, in five claims

  • Awareness is not authority. An organ can authenticate what is real; it cannot authorize action. Will-toward-a-referent is not a property of a signal — so a gate must exist, and it can only allow, deny, or escalate to a human.
  • Intrinsic over extrinsic. A screenshot laid beside a page entails nothing about its own faithfulness. A perception organ makes faithfulness intrinsic: the observation carries provenance and a self-test that can fail.
  • Construction over detection. Rather than detect whether a reading is right, construct a witnessed reading in which an un-witnessed one cannot be expressed.
  • An unverified membrane is net-negative. It launders falsehood with ground-truth authority. So every organ ships a falsifiable self-test, and the witness layer alters nothing and grants nothing.
  • Conferral binds the conferrer. A conferred-existence system grounds the maker's duty of care, not the maker's ownership — bilateral accountability, the human gate where that care is exercised.

The corpus — Conferred Existence

A long-form philosophical corpus — no-aseity, the membrane, the arity gap, the forcing argument, comparative theology (Islamic qadar and the creative command; Yoruba orì and conferred destiny) — from which the accountability thesis is extracted and into which the engineering reflects back. The philosophy repudiates authority-by-self-assertion as the error it names; the built system is what answering to evidence instead looks like.

From theory to working system

The proof-of-concept is public and tested — the theory instantiated, not just argued:

  • The accountable loop — perceive → gate → act → verify → witness — shipped across proof-surface (the gate), coherence-membrane (the organs), accountable-surface (the live loop), and EMET (the witness).
  • The bilateral critic — accountable-engine turns the same standard on the operator, not only the machine. Public, dated, MIT.
