The floor

Looking verified is not the same as being verifiable.

Most software asks you to trust its own report. The system grades its own homework and shows you the grade. That can look airtight and still be wrong, because the claim and the check come from the same place.

The fix is plain: work the answer out yourself, from outside the thing making the claim. Re-derive it, re-run it, compare. If it holds, you have a proof you can keep. If it cannot be checked, the honest answer is to say so.

That is the accountable surface everything here stands on. Not a feature bolted on at the end, the floor the whole line is built on.
