Research thesis

AI systems should keep receipts where humans can actually read them.

That thesis shows up in WARDEN Sensorium for live-state receipts, WARDEN Agent Audit for intent/action review, WARDEN Release Assurance for public-release gates, and EMET for source/view comparison. No trust verdict, no magic authority, just witness records another system can check.

Approach

How the work gets made.

My path is nontraditional: arborist work, Xbox networking support, and then a long self-directed sprint across compilers, graphics, color science, frontend sites, compliance surfaces, and AI tooling.

A lot of the work came from late nights and private iteration. The public standard is still ordinary engineering evidence: repos, commands, tests, examples, diffs, and clear maturity labels.

The through-line is simple: build the thing, label its limits, and leave enough evidence for another person to judge it.
