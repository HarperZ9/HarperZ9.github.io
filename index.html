Approach

How the work gets made.

I spent eleven years as a commercial arborist in a father-son business. Somewhere between the climbing rope and the chainsaw, I taught myself to write software, and never stopped.

Light into color profiles. Reality into real-time renders. Human intent into running code. I am neurodivergent, and rather than fight it, I built my whole workflow around it: directing agentic AI coding tools across design, build, test, and integration, then verifying and owning every line. Less prompt-and-pray, more conducting an orchestra where every player is a model. Pattern-finding and stubborn iteration in place of credentials — and somehow it ships real software.

One thread runs through all of it: how things cross from organic to analog to digital, across language and media.
