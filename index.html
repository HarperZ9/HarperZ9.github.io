Looking verified is not the same as being verifiable.

Proving who you are is not permission to act. A practice run on old data is not money earned. A certificate on the wall is not safety. The fix is the same everywhere: work the answer out yourself, from outside the thing making the claim. Three flagship engines carry that idea into working code, and everything here has a name, a date, and a source you can check.

Each one stands alone. Each links straight to its own source. None asks for your trust.

three flagships · public and tested · authored and dated

Three engines, one discipline.

index draws your whole multi-repo workspace from evidence and checks it against the architecture you meant, then hands back a MATCH, DRIFT, or UNVERIFIABLE verdict you re-run instead of trust. Nine ecosystems, fully offline, zero dependencies. Public on PyPI as index-graph.

forum orchestrates many agents and keeps a witnessed causal ledger of who did what and why, so a multi-agent run is accountable instead of opaque.

telos is the surface where a person and a model perceive, generate, and verify together. It is where the witness and the gate finally shake hands: it signs every drawing it makes, and the gate decides whether one handed back is allowed in.

The three are peers. Each does a distinct job, and each opens its own source.
