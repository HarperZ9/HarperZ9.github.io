The line

Seven engines, equal standing.

Each one earns its place the same way: it shows its work and lets you check it. Together they are the surface a person and a model build on without anyone having to be trusted.

the telos engine

perceive and make active · first flagship

The shared surface where a person and a model perceive the same thing, shape it, and check every step. The first flagship, the engine this whole line is named for.

index

map and verify shipped · PyPI

Draws your whole multi-repo workspace from evidence and checks it against the architecture you meant. You get back a MATCH, DRIFT, or UNVERIFIABLE certificate you re-run instead of trust. Nine ecosystems, fully offline, zero dependencies.

gather

intake and witness shipped · PyPI

The research-intake organ. It reaches information anywhere, including the hard places: gated APIs, paywalls, JavaScript-walled pages, scanned PDFs, audio, facts scattered across fragments. Every item carries a provenance receipt, and a run emits a witnessed digest the other engines consume.

forum

orchestrate v1.12.0 · fair-source

Runs many agents and keeps a witnessed causal ledger of who did what and why. A multi-agent run becomes a record you read back, step by step, instead of an opaque box.

Open › GitHub

crucible

judge 1.1.0 · release candidate

The cognition organ. It registers a thesis, steelmans each claim, measures what holds against a substrate, and refines the weakest axis until the verdict is earned. MATCH, DRIFT, or UNVERIFIABLE, grounded in a measurement rather than a judge's say-so.

Open › GitHub

emet

witness v0.2.0 · three languages

The external byte witness. It re-derives a file's bytes from scratch and reports MATCH, DRIFT, or UNVERIFIABLE, never the word trusted. It alters nothing and grants nothing, the witnessing move on its own, reproduced in three independent languages.

Open › GitHub

buildlang

author v1.0.0 · compiler

A typed-effects systems language. Functions declare the effects they may perform and the lifetimes of the references they return, checked by the compiler and lowered through a verified C path. The language the rest of the stack can be authored in, where every effect is on the record.

The floor

The floor

Looking verified is not the same as being verifiable.

It started as a way to catch an AI in a confident lie. The same gap turned out to be everywhere: proving who you are is not permission to act, a practice run on old data is not money earned, a certificate on the wall is not safety. Something that looks verified, mistaken for the thing it is meant to guarantee.

Every engine here runs the same move. Go back to the evidence, work the answer out again, stay honest about what you could not check, and give one of three verdicts: MATCH, DRIFT, or UNVERIFIABLE. Never the word trusted, never a guess dressed as an answer. index is live now: pip install index-graph, the whole map and certificate in one command.

Build it to be checked, or do not ship it.
