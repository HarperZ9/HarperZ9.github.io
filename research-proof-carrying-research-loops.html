Proof-Carrying Research Loops

A stricter format for using Telos tools to advance research without promoting weak evidence.

Zain Dana Harper/ Seattle · 2026/ draft · not archive-submitted/ research index

Status

This is a working paper draft. The general program claim is a HYPOTHESIS, the local pandas reproduction is a PROBE_MATCH, and paper/source intake remains SOURCE_LEAD. No new theorem, natural law, official submission, or publication acceptance is claimed here.

Promotion ladder

SOURCE_LEAD
A paper, issue, dataset, repository, or source body worth examining.
HYPOTHESIS
A plausible claim with a named falsifier or missing experiment.
IDENTITY
A mathematical or system identity with a proof sketch, derivation, or executable model.
PROBE_MATCH
A bounded local computation matched the expected behavior.
CRUCIBLE_MATCH
Crucible assessed a claim as matching a measurement packet.
UNVERIFIABLE
Evidence is insufficient, inaccessible, or out of scope.
LAW_CANDIDATE
A repeatable invariant with clear scope and falsification.
PROMOTED_LAW
Reserved for independently checked work. None are promoted here.

Method

The research loop is tool-native, not prose-native.

  • Gather captures source candidates and keeps them as source leads until stronger evidence exists.
  • Index bounds the workspace context and source references.
  • Forum routes the intent and review vocabulary.
  • Crucible compares claims to measurements and returns MATCH, DRIFT, or UNVERIFIABLE.
  • Learn turns proof packets into durable skill acquisition and next-study loops.
  • Telos packages the loop into action receipts, research packets, and public handoffs.

Publication rule

Every official paper should carry a website copy and an archive-ready copy. Both must preserve the same status labels, evidence paths, missing-evidence boundaries, and recheck commands. A later stronger receipt revises a paper explicitly; it does not silently overwrite the earlier claim.
