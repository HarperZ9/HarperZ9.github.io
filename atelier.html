ATELIER · ALGORITHMIC DRAWINGS

Living structure, drawn by algorithm.

I photograph small living things — a snail’s logarithmic shell, the curl of a spent seedhead, the veins of a mallow. Here those forms seed algorithms that draw: phyllotaxis, flow fields, differential growth, venation, reaction–diffusion. Every line is generated live in your browser — nothing on this page is a saved image.

The same seed always draws the same thing. Run it, steer it with a photograph, export the vector — the drawing carries its own provenance.

In-browser · zero dependencies · deterministic from seed · export to a witnessed, plot-optimised SVG

The instrument.

Pick an algorithm and a specimen. The photograph is read as a field of light — the algorithm bends to it, so the snail’s whorl or the mallow’s veins surface in the strokes. Drag the complexity, draw a new seed, change the pens. When you find one you like, export it. It comes out as a clean plot file — touching strokes stitched into continuous pen paths, ordered to lift the pen as little as possible and grouped by pen, then stamped with a witness hash: the exact file re-derives from its seed, and says in its own metadata what it optimised.

This instrument needs JavaScript — it generates every line at runtime. The algorithms are plain, dependency-free code in system/atelier.js.

No libraries, no saved frames — a small seeded random-number generator, the five algorithms, a canvas, and a plot optimiser that stitches and orders the lines for a real pen. Why it belongs here: a drawing that is deterministic from a stated seed, and carries a witness of its own geometry, is one you can re-derive instead of trust — the same move the rest of this work makes, in a form you can hang on a wall.

Verify — re-derive it, don’t trust it.

Every exported file carries its seed and a witness of its own geometry. Drop one back here: the instrument rebuilds the drawing from scratch, recomputes the witness, and reports MATCH if the file is exactly what its seed produces, DRIFT if a single coordinate was changed, or UNVERIFIABLE if it can’t read a seed to check against. The same move the witness makes on a file’s bytes — here, on a drawing.

The re-derivation is the proof: same seed, same algorithm, same photograph → the same geometry and the same witness, every time. A DRIFT means the file on disk is no longer what the seed makes — edited, re-encoded, or produced by a different build. Nothing here trusts the file’s own say-so; it rebuilds, and checks.

Five algorithms, four photographs.

Each is a real generative method with a long lineage in mathematics and plotter art — chosen because a specimen in the work already embodies it.

Phyllotaxisfrom the snail Vogel’s model: a seed placed every golden angle of turn, radius growing as the square root of its index. The same logarithmic packing a snail builds its shell by — and the Fibonacci spiral arms emerge on their own.
Flow fieldfrom the dandelion Thousands of particles traced through a field of angles drawn from layered noise, bent toward the light in the photograph — so the strokes radiate the way the ray-florets do, then break into turbulence.
Differential growthfrom the seedhead A closed curve that pushes away from itself and lengthens, inserting new points where it stretches — the same buckling that curls a drying seedhead into folds. You watch it convolve in real time.
Venationfrom the mallow Space colonisation: veins grow from a root toward scattered sources of auxin, thickening as their subtree grows — the algorithm botanists use to model exactly the leaf-veins the mallow wears.
Reaction–diffusionfrom the katydids Gray–Scott: two chemicals diffuse at different rates while one feeds on the other, and the field self-organises into Turing patterns — the mathematics behind spots, mazes, and a katydid’s speckle. Drawn as iso-contours; the photograph’s light sets which regime forms where.
