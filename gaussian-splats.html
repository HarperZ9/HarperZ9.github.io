What counts as a splat.

The acceptance boundary is intentionally stricter than visual resemblance.

A scene artifact exists.

The result must include a loadable spatial file, planned as .spz. A PNG, video, or still render alone is not the scene.

The camera changes the evidence.

Translation must produce meaningful parallax, changing occlusion, and a different spatial relationship among visible elements.

The source remains visible.

The original artwork stays canonical and is shown beside the generated scene. The experiment does not overwrite or silently “upgrade” it.

The invention is disclosed.

The record must identify the generator commit, provider and model versions, parameters, output hash, and inferred backsides, holes, floating fragments, or duplicated forms.

Current boundary.

Source selection and receipts are complete. Scene generation is not.

No Gaussian-splat scene is being represented as finished on this page. The three source PNGs and their hashes are prepared. Actual generation through neilsonnn/image-blaster requires the upstream service credentials to be configured locally or in a protected secret store. A single-image result will be described as a generated spatial interpretation, not a scan or measured reconstruction.
