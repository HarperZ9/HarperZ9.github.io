Zain Dana HarperRAW · the rendering branch

Take the pipeline. Render the frame yourself.

RAW — Rendering Advancement Workshop — is a real-time D3D11 rendering platform: a proxy DLL that takes ownership of the graphics pipeline and dispatches its own effects mid-frame — screen-space global illumination, ambient occlusion, reflections, skylighting. It is the deepest reverse-engineering and graphics work I’ve done, and the branch that becomes the substrate’s spatial sense.

I learned this owning a frame that wasn’t mine. I’m turning it toward a visual engine that is.

RAW 1.0.0 · C++23 · HLSL · D3D11 proxy + mid-frame dispatch

You don’t get the pipeline. So you take it.

A closed real-time renderer hands you no seam to work in. RAW makes one: a d3d11.dll proxy wraps the Device, Context, and SwapChain, a DSV-unbind heuristic classifies the frame into nine render phases as it happens, and RAW injects its own work at PostGeometry — inside the live frame, not bolted on after it. That is the whole feat: ownership of a pipeline you were never given the source to, earned by reverse-engineering how it draws.

Phase detection, mid-frame dispatch, a depth pyramid rebuilt from the host’s buffers — this is where I learned the reverse-engineering and memory discipline that runs under the rest of the work.

Screen-space GI, AO, reflections and skylighting, each implementing a named technique from the literature — and each carrying its real state, including the two that don’t look right yet. Proof before trust applies to my own renderer.

Architecture: a HiZ pyramid (standard-to-reversed-Z with a mip chain), a 9-category weather system that auto-modulates effect intensity, and a post chain (Karis+Jiménez bloom, an AgX color pipeline, auto-exposure) currently in build, untested. 17 further renderers — DoF, volumetrics, SSS, clouds, SDSM — are written and source-preserved but removed from the build pending individual polish. Stated plainly, not implied.

Two shapes: where it came from, where it’s going.

The origin was a full rendering platform — and where I forged a specific set of skills: reverse-engineering a closed pipeline, reading and re-deriving GPU memory and buffer state mid-frame, and the discipline of making an injected effect behave inside a frame it doesn’t own. Those are the same instincts the witness and provenance work elsewhere here is built on; RAW is where I learned them the hard way.

I learned this owning a frame that wasn’t mine. I’m turning it toward a visual engine that is.

The direction is the substrate’s spatial sense. Agentic tools are still mostly blind to live, spatial, rendered state — they reason over text and screenshots, not over the frame as it is being drawn. RAW is the branch I am pointing at that gap: native, real-time integration that lets a model perceive and reason about spatial and live rendered state the way the accountability organs let it perceive files and the web. The same lineage, given a new shape and a new purpose — and possibly its own line as it matures into the visual engine of the larger work.

Status, honestly: the rendering platform is in active development — core effects functional, two in repair, the post chain untested. The spatial-engine direction is frontier roadmap, not a shipped claim.
