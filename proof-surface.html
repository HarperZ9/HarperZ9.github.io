ACCOUNTABILITY INFRASTRUCTURE · PROOF SURFACE

A grant of authority should expire, be traced, and say no.

Proof Surface is a stdlib-only accountability contract: an evidence packet with provenance, a work-record receipt that flows outward for review, an authorization receipt that records a real grant from a real human, and a pre-execution gate that is default-deny and fail-closed. A delegation chain where every hop attenuates its parent’s scope — widening is the shape of privilege escalation, and it is refused. Verdicts live in closed lattices; nothing here emits TRUSTED or APPROVED.

Advisory by design — a recommendation the runtime enforces, not an authority the library grants.

v0.1.0 · stdlib-only · zero dependencies · 258 tests

Accountability made structural, not aspirational.

A claim about accountability that grants itself authority would refute itself. Proof Surface takes the opposite position: every validator rejects authority-shaped content; every verdict is a closed enumeration the caller can check; every decision helper returns a structured result — allow, deny, needs-human, deploy, block — and leaves enforcement to the runtime or the operator. The library is the evidence record. The library is not the enforcer.

Two receipts, deliberately separate. The work-record receipt flows outward — a verifiable record of what an agent did, for review. The authorization receipt flows inward — a real grant from a real human principal, hard-requiring an expiry and an explicit allowlist of permitted actions. Empty allowed-actions authorizes nothing. Authority must be stated, scoped, and timed. The same forbidden-field guard runs recursively at every object level of every contract.

The gate withholds approval unless authorization, budget, and observed state each positively pass — default-deny, fail-closed, advisory.

The contract family.

Proof-surface packet — a neutral evidence and index packet that producers emit and a proof-index consumes; the structural spine the other contracts attach to.

Work-record receipt — a verifiable record of agent work that flows outward for review. Direction is fixed to output-only; a work record is emitted, never read back as inbound model or session state.

Authorization receipt — a verifiable record of a real, explicit, least-privilege, expiring, revocable grant of authority from a human principal to an agent. Verifier input only — never re-injected as model context. check_action confirms a specific action against a receipt; it does not inject trusted state into a model.

Pre-execution gate — a default-deny, fail-closed, advisory mediation layer. Given a planned action, its authorization receipt, a budget, and optional observed state, it returns a GateDecision (allow / deny / needs-human) with per-dimension check results. Allow is the rarest outcome. Any dimension that cannot be positively confirmed escalates to needs-human rather than auto-allowing.

Evaluation contract — evaluation as a deploy gate, not a vanity score. Criteria carry thresholds and a required flag; evaluate returns deploy / block / needs-human and accounts for uncertainty. A result whose interval straddles its threshold escalates to needs-human; a required criterion with no result is missing — needs-human. Never deploys on uncertainty.

Claim ledger — traceable multi-agent memory. Each claim carries a source-provided confidence and explicit depends-on and conflicts-with links, with referential integrity enforced. The ledger surfaces low-confidence claims, declared conflicts, and the transitive set contaminated by a given claim. It reports provenance and uncertainty; it does not adjudicate truth.

Delegation chain — a chain of delegation hops rooted in a real human. The root hop’s from must be a human — authority cannot originate with an agent. Each hop’s scope monotonically attenuates its parent’s; a delegate widening its own actions or targets is the literal shape of privilege escalation and is DENIED. Each hop is hash-chained (SHA-256) and the whole chain is committed into one chain_binding so truncation and extension are caught. verify_delegation returns a closed VALID / DENIED / UNVERIFIABLE verdict; effective_scope is populated only on VALID.

Don’t take the contract. Watch it refuse.

The gate and the delegation chain are the two places authority is checked. These cases are drawn from the real validators — inputs that should be refused, and the verdicts they receive.

Exhibit I Proof Surface · the gate is default-deny and fail-closed
# a gate request (dict) whose authorization receipt is absent
>>> evaluate_gate(request)
GateDecision(decision="deny")
  authorization: no receipt — deny
  budget:        unknown — needs-human
  state:         no observation — needs-human

# an authorization receipt whose allowed_actions list is empty
>>> check_action(receipt, "write_file")
False   # empty allowlist authorizes nothing

# a receipt dict missing expires_at — validation rejects it
>>> validate_authorization_receipt({"allowed_actions": ["write_file"]})
invalid: "expires_at" required — authority must expire

$ pytest
258 passed in 0.26s

No receipt means deny. Empty allowlist means deny. No expiry means the receipt is invalid before the gate ever sees it. Allow is earned by passing every check — not by the absence of a refusal.

Honest limit: the gate is advisory — a structured recommendation; the runtime or operator is the enforcement point. The keyless hash-chain in the delegation contract is self-consistent integrity, NOT tamper-evidence against an adversary who recomputes it; real anti-forgery needs an external anchor (pin chain_binding out-of-band, or verify an asymmetric signature). Demanding signature assurance with no verifier returns UNVERIFIABLE, never a fabricated pass.

Exhibit II Proof Surface · delegation chain refuses privilege escalation
# root hop: human grants agent-A a scope of ["read_log"]
# second hop: agent-A tries to delegate ["read_log", "write_config"] — wider
>>> verify_delegation(chain)
DENIED   # hop 1 scope is not a subset of hop 0 — privilege escalation

# a chain whose root from-field is an agent, not a human
>>> verify_delegation(chain_rooted_in_agent)
DENIED   # root hop must be a human — authority cannot originate with an agent

# a valid chain: human → agent-A ["read_log"] → agent-B ["read_log"]
>>> verify_delegation(valid_chain)
VALID    effective_scope = ["read_log"]

A delegate cannot widen its own authority. The root must be a human. effective_scope is returned only on VALID — a DENIED or UNVERIFIABLE result carries no scope.

Action and target matching is exact and case-sensitive because real resource identifiers are. What it is, and what it is not.

Proof Surface is a set of validators and decision helpers — stdlib Python, zero external dependencies, 258 passing tests. Every contract is a closed lattice; no verdict is fabricated; no validator grants authority. The forbidden-field guard runs recursively so authority-shaped content cannot slip through a nested object that the top-level schema misses.

Said plainly: the gate is advisory. The library recommends; the runtime enforces. A gate that declared itself the enforcer and ran in the same process as the thing it was gating would not be a gate. The library is the contract and the evidence record — enforcement belongs in the caller’s hands, where it can be inspected and replaced.

One author, one implementation, stdlib-only — the named edges of a first working contract, not a complete production enforcement layer.

The claim ledger keeps confidence honest: a 0.0 confidence claim is logged, not dropped or adjusted. The evaluation contract does not deploy on uncertainty: a result whose measured interval straddles its threshold escalates, never passes. The delegation chain’s keyless binding detects partial corruption and naive truncation or extension; it does not resist an adversary who rewrites content and recomputes every binding — that boundary is stated, not glossed.
